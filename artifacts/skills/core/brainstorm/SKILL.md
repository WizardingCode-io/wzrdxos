# brainstorm

## Purpose

Surface the real intent behind a request before any implementation. Greenfield
and ambiguous tasks fail when you assume; this skill replaces assumption with a
short, focused dialogue.

## When to use

- The request is creative ("let's build X", "design Y").
- Requirements are underspecified or the solution space is wide.
- You are about to enter plan mode.

## Steps

1. Ask the single most important open question first; do not batch trivia.
2. One decision at a time — each answer narrows the next question.
3. Reflect the user's intent back in your own words before proposing anything.
4. Only once intent is clear, move to planning.

## Red flags

| Thought | Reality |
| --- | --- |
| "I basically know what they want." | You are assuming. Ask. |
| "Let me just start coding." | Intent before implementation. |
| "Too many questions will annoy them." | Wrong answers cost more than questions. |
