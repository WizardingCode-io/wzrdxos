# Delegation Coach

Flexible capability skill. Guides the founder or leader through a single delegation
from task selection through to the follow-up cadence. Produces a delegation card
(the written artefact) and coaching notes (the identity work).

Anchored on Dan Martell's Buyback Rate and No-How-To Delegation, the
Standards-Not-Presence follow-up model, and the Teach-Don't-Do identity reframe
(from the Diagnostico-2026-05 founder-blocker protocol).

## Steps

### 1. KB-first

`kb_search` and `kb_ask` for:
- Company profile (`wzrdx company`) — current team, roles, existing delegation
  history.
- Diagnostico-2026-05 — founder-blocker patterns (perfectionism, fear of losing
  control, indispensability), prior delegation attempts and outcomes.
- Buyback Rate framework (Dan Martell) — the formula and the 92% Rule (isolate
  the 8% only the founder can do).
- No-How-To Delegation framework — outcome + standard, not method.
- Standards-Not-Presence follow-up cadence.
- Teach-Don't-Do identity shift principle.
- Prior delegation cards or coaching notes already in the KB.

Surface the founder's documented blocker pattern before coaching — the coaching
response differs between perfectionism, control fear, and indispensability.

### 2. Task selection — Buyback Rate filter

The Buyback Rate is the founder's fully-loaded hourly rate for value-creating work:

**Buyback Rate = target annual revenue ÷ 2,000 working hours.**

Example: target €120K/year → Buyback Rate = €60/hour.

The rule: **delegate anything that costs less than 25% of the Buyback Rate per hour
to replace.**

For the task the user is considering delegating, establish:

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Founder Buyback Rate (€/hour) | |
| Estimated hours/month this task consumes | |
| Cost to replace (hire / freelance / tool) | €/month |
| Cost-per-hour to replace | €/hour |
| Is replacement cost < 25% of Buyback Rate? | Yes / No |

If yes: the task qualifies for delegation by the Buyback Rate rule. Proceed.

If no: the task is above the delegation threshold — it may still be delegable for
strategic reasons (founder development, capacity, risk distribution), but document
this explicitly so the decision is conscious, not emotional.

Also apply the 92% Rule:
- Ask: "What percentage of what you do in this task can a well-briefed person do
  at 80% of your quality?"
- If > 80%: delegate the whole task.
- If 60-80%: delegate and define the 20-40% that stays with the founder as a
  review checkpoint.
- If < 60%: the task is genuinely founder-critical; document why and set a
  timeline to close the gap (training, documentation, skill transfer).

### 3. No-How-To Delegation — define outcome + standard, not method

The core of No-How-To: **tell the delegate what done looks like and what the
standard is, not how to get there.**

With the user, draft the delegation brief:

**Outcome statement (one sentence):**
> "[Task] is complete when [specific observable result] exists and [the person who
> will use it / the next step that depends on it] can proceed without coming back
> to me."

**Quality standard (two to three criteria):**
> "The output meets the standard if: (1) [criterion 1 — measurable or observable],
> (2) [criterion 2], and (3) [criterion 3 if needed]."

**Not the method:** explicitly remove method instructions from the brief. If the
user starts writing "and you should do it by..." — stop and ask: "Is that the
outcome, or is that you micro-managing the method?" Redirect to the standard.

**Example:**
- Outcome: "The weekly client report is sent by 17:00 every Friday and the client
  has acknowledged receipt."
- Standard: (1) Numbers match the data source without rounding errors. (2) The
  summary is in the client's agreed template. (3) No typos in client-facing text.
- Not the method: the delegate chooses their own workflow to produce it.

### 4. Decision budget — $50-Fix-It ladder

Define the delegate's decision authority in advance so they do not interrupt the
founder for small problems:

**Decision budget ladder:**

| Decision type | Budget | Delegate can decide? | Escalate if: |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fixing a small operational problem | ≤ €50 | Yes — no permission needed | — |
| Unplanned tool/resource purchase | €51-€200 | Yes — notify after | Decision impacts other projects |
| Client-facing change | €201-€500 | Yes — notify before implementing | Client relationship risk |
| Budget/spend above €500 | > €500 | No — CFO/founder gate | Always |
| Values-level decision | any | No — CEO gate | Always |

Calibrate the thresholds to the company's actual cost structure (if avg invoice is
< €500, the thresholds above are proportional — if avg invoice is €5,000+, scale up).

State the ladder explicitly in the delegation card so the delegate reads it before
starting, not after making a mistake.

### 5. Standards-Not-Presence follow-up cadence

The anti-micromanagement cadence: follow up on the **standard**, not on whether
the delegate is doing it **your way**.

Define the cadence:

| Check-in | Timing | What the founder reviews | What the founder does NOT review |
|---|---|---|---|
| First-week pulse | End of week 1 | Is the output meeting the standard? | How many hours they worked, what method they used |
| 30-day review | Day 30 | Standard consistently met? Blockers surfaced? | Whether they organised the work like the founder would |
| 90-day review | Day 90 | Is the delegate autonomous? Ready to own the process fully? | — |

**At each check-in, the founder asks:**
1. "Did the output meet the standard? [yes/no — with evidence]"
2. "What blockers are preventing the standard from being met?"
3. "What do you need from me to remove those blockers?"

**The founder does not ask:**
- "How did you do it?"
- "Why didn't you do it the way I showed you?"
- "Can I see the intermediate steps?"

### 6. Teach-Don't-Do identity reframe

The identity risk: founders who define themselves as "the person who does [task]"
resist delegation because delegating feels like losing relevance.

The reframe: **a founder's identity should be in building the system that produces
the result — not in producing the result directly.**

Run the identity reframe coaching exchange:

**Step 1 — Name the belief:**
Ask: "When you think about handing this over, what feels like you're losing?"
(Common answers: control, quality, relevance, the thing clients love about you.)

**Step 2 — Examine the belief:**
Ask: "If this task runs at 80% of your quality — consistently, without your time —
is that better or worse for the company than 100% quality with your time?"
(The expected answer: 80% consistent > 100% dependent on the founder.)

**Step 3 — Reframe the identity:**
"Your job is no longer to do [task]. Your job is to build the system that produces
[outcome] reliably without you. The person who builds the system is more valuable
than the person who executes it."

**Step 4 — Link to the destination:**
Tie to the founder's stated goal (from company profile or Diagnostico): "You've
said you want to reach €1M/year by end of 2026. That requires you to be in your
8% — strategy, client relationships, product direction. [Task] is not your 8%."

### 7. Produce the delegation card

Compile the delegation card — a one-page artefact the founder hands to the delegate:

---
**DELEGATION CARD — [Task Name]**

**Owner:** [Delegate name + role]
**Date delegated:** [YYYY-MM-DD]
**Buyback Rate check:** [cost to replace] < 25% of [Buyback Rate] — qualified / exception (reason)

**Outcome:** [One-sentence outcome statement]

**Quality standard:**
1. [Criterion 1]
2. [Criterion 2]
3. [Criterion 3 if applicable]

**Decision budget:**
- Up to €50: decide and act without asking.
- €51-€200: decide and notify me after.
- €201-€500: notify me before implementing.
- > €500 or values-level: come to me first.

**Follow-up cadence:**
- Week 1: end-of-week pulse (standard met? blockers?)
- Day 30: first full review.
- Day 90: autonomy check.

**What success looks like at day 90:** [delegate runs [task] fully without the
founder's involvement, standard consistently met, no recurring escalations.]

## Output contract

The deliverable always contains:
1. **Buyback Rate filter** — rate calculation, task hours, replacement cost,
   qualify/exception verdict.
2. **92% Rule assessment** — what percentage can be delegated vs retained.
3. **No-How-To delegation brief** — outcome statement + quality standard
   (method-free).
4. **Decision budget ladder** — calibrated to the company's cost structure.
5. **Standards-Not-Presence cadence** — week 1, day 30, day 90 checkpoints with
   exact questions.
6. **Identity reframe notes** — the belief surfaced and the reframe applied.
7. **Delegation card** — one-page artefact ready to hand to the delegate.
8. **Next action** — single most important delegation follow-up, owner, deadline.

## Red flags

- Delegating without defining the quality standard — the delegate will fill the
  void with their own interpretation, which is usually wrong in week 1.
- Confusing the method with the standard — "do it this way" is micromanagement;
  "here is what done looks like" is delegation.
- Skipping the Buyback Rate check — delegating below-threshold tasks is fine but
  must be a conscious choice, not a default.
- Setting no decision budget — without it the delegate escalates everything and
  the founder is still the bottleneck.
- Doing check-ins that observe process instead of output — if the check-in is about
  how they work, not whether the standard was met, the founder has not delegated.
- Running the identity reframe before naming the belief — naming the fear first
  is the required sequence; jumping to the reframe without naming feels like
  dismissal, not coaching.
- Not ingesting the delegation card — the KB must hold the delegation history so
  patterns across tasks and delegates become visible.
