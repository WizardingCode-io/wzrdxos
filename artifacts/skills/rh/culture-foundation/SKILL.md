# Culture Foundation

Flexible capability skill. Runs a mission/vision/values workshop from the founder
interview through to the culture one-pager and an adoption ritual. Starts by
anchoring in the current cultural baseline before writing anything.

Anchored on Patrick Lencioni's Five Dysfunctions of a Team (the diagnostic layer),
Netflix talent-density and culture deck principles (selectively — adapted for
early-stage PT teams), Sabri Suby's Collect People Doctrine (cultural framing as
a talent magnet), and the Diagnostico-2026-05 Culture 25/100 baseline.

## Steps

### 1. KB-first

`kb_search` and `kb_ask` for:
- Company profile (`wzrdx company`) — current team, stage, history, any prior
  culture artefacts.
- Diagnostico-2026-05 — Cultura 25/100 score, specific cultural failure patterns
  identified (5 failed hires, founder centralisation), D1-D180 roadmap culture
  milestones.
- Five Dysfunctions of a Team (Lencioni) — the pyramid model: absence of trust,
  fear of conflict, lack of commitment, avoidance of accountability, inattention
  to results.
- Netflix Culture Deck principles relevant to early-stage: talent density,
  freedom + responsibility, context not control, adequate performance gets a
  generous severance.
- Collect People Doctrine (Sabri Suby) — the principle that the best people
  attract more best people; culture as the primary talent magnet.
- Any M/V/V drafts or culture documents already in the KB.

Surface the Diagnostico baseline and the five failed hire patterns explicitly —
the culture workshop must reckon with the evidence, not start from a blank page.

### 2. Current state — culture diagnostic

Before drafting M/V/V, establish the current cultural reality:

**Founder interview — ten questions (run with the founder):**
1. "Why does this company exist beyond making money? What problem would you continue
   to solve even if you were not paid to solve it?"
2. "Describe the best person you ever worked with. What did they do that others
   didn't?"
3. "What behaviour would you fire someone for in their first week, even if their
   work was excellent?"
4. "What is one thing clients say about working with us that you most want to
   protect as we grow?"
5. "What is one thing about how we work that you would never trade away, even for
   2× the revenue?"
6. "When you are at your best as a leader in this company, what does that look
   like?"
7. "What has caused the most internal friction in the last 12 months?"
8. "If someone joined the team and thrived here for 2 years, what would be true
   about them?"
9. "If someone joined and failed here in 90 days, what would be true about them?"
10. "What does the company look like in 5 years if everything goes right?"

Record the answers verbatim. Identify recurring themes (3-5 themes are the raw
material for values).

**Five Dysfunctions baseline check (Lencioni):**
Score each dysfunction on a 1-5 scale (1 = severe dysfunction, 5 = healthy)
based on observable evidence from the founder interview and Diagnostico data:

| Dysfunction | Score (1-5) | Evidence |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Absence of trust (willingness to be vulnerable) | | |
| Fear of conflict (productive ideological debate) | | |
| Lack of commitment (clarity and buy-in on decisions) | | |
| Avoidance of accountability (peer pressure on standards) | | |
| Inattention to results (collective results > individual status) | | |

Any dysfunction scoring ≤ 2 must be named in the culture one-pager as a current
risk — culture documents that ignore real dysfunctions are performative.

### 3. Mission statement (Why)

Draft the mission statement from the interview themes:

**Mission = [Verb] + [Who we serve] + [What transformation we create] + [Why it
matters beyond profit].**

Rules:
- One sentence, ≤ 20 words.
- Uses a strong active verb (build, free, transform, equip, protect — not "to be
  a leader in" or "to provide").
- Names the beneficiary, not the company.
- Is checkable: in 12 months, has the company done this?

Draft 3 candidate statements from the interview themes. Present all three to the
founder with a recommendation for the strongest one and the rationale.

### 4. Vision statement (Where)

Draft the vision:

**Vision = the world / company / state that exists when the mission succeeds,
5-10 years from now.**

Rules:
- One to two sentences, ≤ 30 words.
- Specific enough to be verifiably achieved or not (not "we will be the best
  company in X").
- Aspirational but credible given the evidence of what the company can do.
- Internally coherent with the mission.

Draft 2 candidates. Present with recommendation.

### 5. Values (How we behave)

Extract 3-5 values from the interview themes. For each value:

**Name:** one word or short phrase.
**Meaning:** one sentence that defines what this value looks like in practice.
**Behaviour examples:**
- "We show this value when..." (two examples of the value in action).
**Counter-examples:**
- "This value is violated when..." (one clear violation example, drawn from the
  Diagnostico or interview if possible).

Rules:
- Values must be behaviourally specific — not aspirational adjectives.
- "Honesty" alone is not a value; "We tell clients the truth before they ask,
  even when the news is bad" is a value.
- Every value must have at least one counter-example or it cannot be used for
  hiring, performance, or exit decisions.
- The Netflix "adequate performance gets generous severance" principle applies to
  any value that pertains to performance standards: if we say "excellence", we must
  be able to act on it.

Test: for each value, ask "would we make a hiring, promotion, or exit decision
based on a violation of this value?" If the answer is no, the value is decorative —
remove it or make it specific enough to use.

### 6. Collect People Doctrine — culture as talent magnet

Sabri Suby's Collect People Doctrine: the most powerful recruiting tool is a
culture that exceptional people want to join, stay in, and refer others to.

Evaluate the draft M/V/V against three Collect People criteria:
1. **Does this culture attract the person you described in question 2 of the
   founder interview?** — If not, adjust. The M/V/V must be the answer to "why
   would the best person in the market choose us over a bigger company?"
2. **Does this culture repel the wrong people explicitly?** — A culture that sounds
   appealing to everyone is not a culture; it is a marketing tagline.
3. **Would an exceptional person be proud to say they work here?** — The values
   must be worth sharing publicly, not just internally.

State the Collect People verdict: does the M/V/V pass all three criteria? If not,
flag which criterion fails and what adjustment is needed.

### 7. Internal NPS baseline

Establish a culture-health baseline via internal NPS:

**Single question:** "On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend working
here to a friend who is looking for a job like yours?"

Target: internal NPS ≥ 8 (net promoter = % 9-10 minus % 0-6).

For a solo founder or a 1-2 person team: skip the NPS (n=1 is not a survey);
instead, record the founder's own score and use the first hire's score at day 90
as the first data point.

For a team of ≥ 3: run the survey before publishing the M/V/V so the current-state
score is the baseline. Run again at day 90 after the M/V/V is published and the
adoption ritual has run.

### 8. Culture one-pager

Produce the culture one-pager — the single document that captures:

---
**[Company name] — Culture Foundation**
*As of [YYYY-MM-DD]*

**Mission:** [one sentence]

**Vision:** [one to two sentences]

**Values:**
1. [Name] — [meaning sentence]. We show this when: [example 1]; [example 2].
   This is violated when: [counter-example].
2. [repeat for each value]

**Five Dysfunctions baseline:** [table from step 2 — scores and evidence]
**Current cultural risks:** [any dysfunction scoring ≤ 2, named plainly]

**Internal NPS baseline:** [score or "first data point at day 90"]
---

### 9. Adoption ritual

A culture document that is not practiced is not culture — it is branding.

Define the minimum viable adoption ritual:

**Weekly ritual (existing team, ≤ 5 min):**
- At the start of the weekly team meeting: one person names a moment in the last
  week where a company value showed up (or failed to show up). No evaluation —
  just the story.
- Rotate ownership of this moment weekly.

**Hiring ritual:**
- Every interview includes at least one values-alignment question tied to a specific
  value (with the counter-example as the disqualifier signal).
- Every offer letter references the M/V/V and the autonomy expectations.

**Exit ritual:**
- Any exit decision must name the values dimension that was not met (or note
  explicitly that the exit is performance-based, not values-based).
- This feeds the Five Dysfunctions tracker and the hiring postmortem log.

**90-day rhythm:**
- At day 90: rerun internal NPS. Compare to baseline.
- If NPS has not moved, review whether the adoption ritual is actually running.
- Route the result to the CEO and to the KB via `kb_ingest`.

### 10. Ingest to KB

After the workshop, ingest via `kb_ingest`:

- Source path: `memos/rh/culture-foundation-<company-slug>-<YYYY-MM>`.

- Include: the full culture one-pager, the Five Dysfunctions baseline, the NPS
  baseline, and the adoption ritual definition.

## Output contract

The deliverable always contains:
1. **Five Dysfunctions baseline** — scored table with evidence per dysfunction;
   current cultural risks named explicitly.
2. **Mission** — 3 candidates with recommendation.
3. **Vision** — 2 candidates with recommendation.
4. **Values** — 3-5 values, each with meaning, examples, and counter-example.
5. **Collect People verdict** — 3-criteria assessment; adjustment if failing.
6. **Internal NPS baseline** — score or first-data-point plan.
7. **Culture one-pager** — the final compiled artefact, ready to publish.
8. **Adoption ritual** — weekly, hiring, exit, and 90-day rhythm.
9. **Next action** — single most important culture-activation action, owner,
   deadline within 30 days.

## Red flags

- Writing M/V/V before running the founder interview — the artefacts must come
  from evidence, not generic templates.
- Producing values without counter-examples — a value you cannot violate is not
  a value; it is decoration.
- Ignoring the Five Dysfunctions baseline — culture documents that do not
  acknowledge real dysfunction are marketing materials, not management tools.
- Using more than 5 values — beyond 5, nobody remembers them; they collapse
  into "be good at everything."
- Skipping the adoption ritual — a document without a practice is inert.
- Applying Netflix culture principles wholesale to a 1-3 person PT company —
  Netflix's "adequate performance gets severance" works at 10,000 employees;
  at 3, it is a fire risk; adopt selectively and name which principles apply now.
- Not ingesting the one-pager — the culture document must live in the KB so
  hiring pipeline, onboarding, and performance reviews can reference it.
