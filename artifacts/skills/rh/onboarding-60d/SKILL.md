# Onboarding 60d

Flexible capability skill. Produces and tracks the full 60-day onboarding plan from
day 1 through to declared autonomy. Calibrated against the Diagnostico D61-D90 hire
target and the D180 autonomy milestone (24-Oct-2026).

This skill is executed **jointly with the COO** — the CHRO owns the people/culture
side (attitude, coaching, feedback loops, values alignment); the COO owns the
capacity/process side (task assignment, SOPs/POPs, operational checkpoints, KPI
tracking). Coordinate explicitly at the weekly checkpoint.

Anchored on the three-repetition rule and interrupt-correct-repeat coaching loop
(Dan Martell), and the observe → do together → supervised → autonomous framework.

## Steps

### 1. KB-first

`kb_search` and `kb_ask` for:
- Company profile (`wzrdx company`) — team, active processes, open roles.
- Prior onboarding plans or records for any role already in the KB — use as scaffold,
  adapt to the new hire's role and context.
- The 5 failed hires postmortem (Diagnostico D21) — were any failures attributable
  to onboarding gaps? Surface those lessons explicitly.
- Relevant frameworks: the three-repetition rule, interrupt-correct-repeat, Buyback
  Rate, No-How-To Delegation, First-Week Leadership Diagnostic.
- COO's current SOP/POP inventory — which processes does the new hire need to own?
  Pull the relevant POPs from the KB to include in the onboarding plan.

Note what already exists. Only create new artefacts for genuine gaps.

### 2. Onboarding plan structure

Produce the full 60-day onboarding plan before the hire starts.

**Plan header:**

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Hire name / role | |
| Start date | |
| Autonomy target date | D+60 (or D+90 for complex roles) |
| CHRO lead | (people/culture side) |
| COO lead | (process/capacity side) |
| Weekly checkpoint day | (e.g. every Friday) |

**Success definition (agreed before day 1):**
What does "autonomous" mean for this role? Write it as measurable outcomes:
- Can execute [core process] without supervision.
- Owns [metric / KPI] and reports it weekly.
- Has completed [specific deliverable] at acceptable quality.
- Passes the 90%+ quality bar on [case or scenario].

### 3. Phase 1 — Week 1: Observe (shadowing)

Goal: the new hire observes everything. They do not own tasks yet.

**Day 1:**
- Welcome, culture brief (mission/vision/values — even if not yet documented,
  articulate them verbally and note them for documentation).
- Team introductions — structure them; do not leave this to chance.
- Overview of the tools, systems and access needed.
- First impression diagnostic (CHRO): attitude, curiosity, communication style.
  Note observations, do not give feedback yet.

**Days 2-5:**
- Shadow every core process relevant to the role (COO provides the process list).
- New hire takes notes; does not intervene or execute yet.
- End-of-day 5: CHRO check-in (15 min) — "What have you observed? What surprised
  you? What questions do you have?" Listen more than talk. Assess initiative and
  cultural fit signals.
- COO check-in: confirm the process/tool list is complete and access is working.

**Phase 1 output:** CHRO and COO share written observations. Flag any early concerns
to each other within 48h — do not wait for the weekly checkpoint.

### 4. Phase 2 — Week 2: Do together (pair)

Goal: the new hire does the work with the founder/manager alongside — no solo
execution yet.

**Each core process:**
1. Explain the what and the why (not just the how — this is No-How-To Delegation:
   the new hire must understand the goal, not memorise steps).
2. Do it together: the new hire leads, the founder corrects in real time using the
   interrupt-correct-repeat loop (see step 7).
3. Debrief immediately after: "What went well? What would you do differently?"

**Three-repetition rule (Dan Martell):** when a behaviour or step is corrected,
it is not learned until it occurs correctly three times in a row. Track corrections
explicitly — do not move on from a step until the new hire has executed it correctly
three consecutive times.

**End-of-week 2 checkpoint (CHRO + COO):**
- What processes can move to supervised execution?
- What needs more paired repetitions?
- Any attitude or culture signals to address?

### 5. Phase 3 — Week 3: Supervised execution

Goal: the new hire executes independently; the founder reviews output (not process).

**How to run supervised execution:**
- New hire executes core processes solo.
- Founder reviews the output (not watching over their shoulder) and gives written
  feedback within 24h.
- Feedback format: specific, behavioural, not personal. "The client brief missed
  the budget constraint from the brief — include it next time." Not "you need to
  be more careful."
- Praise public / correct private: recognise wins in front of the team (even if the
  team is small); corrections are 1:1 and immediate.

**Three-repetition rule continues:** any correction during supervised execution
resets the counter for that behaviour. It is not fixed until it recurs correctly
three times.

**End-of-week 3 checkpoint (CHRO + COO):**
- Scorecard: which processes are at autonomous quality (90%+)?
- Which processes need extended supervised execution (go back to paired for those)?
- Flag any patterns: is the new hire asking for help appropriately? Are they hiding
  blockers? Are they showing initiative?

### 6. Phase 4 — Weeks 4-8: Autonomous + weekly checkpoints

Goal: the new hire owns their work. The founder reviews outputs on a scheduled
cadence, not in real time.

**Autonomy contract (set at week 4 start):**
| Process | Autonomy level | Review cadence | Escalation trigger |
|---------|---------------|----------------|--------------------|
| Core process 1 | Full autonomy | Weekly review | [define threshold] |
| Core process 2 | Full autonomy | Weekly review | [define threshold] |
| Complex decision X | Notify before | Per occurrence | Always |

**Weekly checkpoint (30 min, CHRO + COO + new hire):**
1. New hire presents: what was accomplished, what was blocked, what is next.
2. CHRO reviews: attitude, cultural fit, feedback quality, coaching receptiveness.
3. COO reviews: process execution quality, KPI ownership, escalation behaviour.
4. Alignment: any corrections? Any recognition? One thing to improve next week.

**Interrupt-correct-repeat during weeks 4-8:**
If a behaviour or output falls below the quality bar during autonomous phase, do
not wait for the weekly checkpoint:
1. Interrupt: address it immediately, 1:1.
2. Correct: specific, behavioural, with a clear standard. "Next time, do X."
3. Repeat: ask them to replay it (or the next equivalent instance) immediately.
Track: did the correction stick for 3 consecutive occurrences?

**30-day autonomy check (mid-phase):**
- Run the First-Week Leadership Diagnostic scores again (initiative, judgment,
  cultural fit, communication, learning speed — scored 1-5).
- If any dimension is ≤ 2, produce a coaching plan with explicit milestones and a
  "resolve or exit" decision by week 8.
- If all dimensions ≥ 3, confirm trajectory to full autonomy.

### 7. Interrupt-correct-repeat coaching loop

This loop applies across all phases whenever behaviour deviates from the standard.

**Three steps, always in this order:**
1. **Interrupt:** stop the behaviour in the moment (or within the same work session
   if not possible). Do not let it repeat unchallenged — every unchallenged
   deviation sets a new standard.
2. **Correct:** state the standard, not the emotion. "The correct way is [X] because
   [reason]." One correction per session — do not stack multiple corrections.
3. **Repeat:** ask the person to demonstrate the correct behaviour immediately.
   "Can you show me how you would do that now?" This is not punitive — it is
   neurologically how motor and behavioural learning works.

**Three-repetition rule:** the loop closes when the behaviour occurs correctly three
consecutive times without prompting. Track this explicitly. Until then, it is a
work-in-progress, not a resolved issue.

### 8. Success metrics

| Metric | Target | Check date |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Time-to-autonomy | ≤ 60 days (≤ 90 for complex roles) | D+60 |
| 90-day retention | Hire still active and autonomous | D+90 |
| D180 full autonomy | No founder involvement in core processes | D+180 |
| Internal NPS (new hire) | ≥ 8 | D+60 check-in |
| Quality bar (core processes) | ≥ 90% of output passes without correction | D+60 |

### 9. Ingest the onboarding record

At D+60 (or at close of each phase), ingest to the KB via `kb_ingest`:
- Title: `Onboarding — [Role] — [Company] — [YYYY-MM-DD start]`.
- Tags: `onboarding`, `integração`, `<role>`, `<company-slug>`, `rh`, `ops`.
- Include: the full plan, weekly checkpoint notes, correction log, three-repetition
  tracking, D+60 scorecard, autonomy contract, and lessons learned.
- For hires that do not reach autonomy: document the root cause and link to the
  Diagnostico D21 postmortem to build the pattern library and prevent repetition.

## Output contract

The deliverable for each new hire contains:
1. **Onboarding plan** — header, success definition, phase-by-phase schedule.
2. **Process ownership map** — which processes the hire owns and at what autonomy
   level by when.
3. **Correction log template** — three-repetition tracking per behaviour.
4. **Weekly checkpoint agenda** — standard 30-min format for CHRO + COO + hire.
5. **D+60 scorecard** — autonomy check against success metrics.
6. **Next action** — single most important onboarding action right now, owner,
   deadline.

## Red flags

- Starting the onboarding without a written plan and success definition agreed before
  day 1 — the plan exists to make expectations explicit, not to be filled in after
  the fact.
- Skipping phases (e.g. going straight to autonomous) — the observe → pair →
  supervised → autonomous sequence is not optional; each phase builds the foundation
  for the next.
- Treating the three-repetition rule as bureaucratic — it is the difference between
  "I told them" and "they learned it."
- Giving feedback without the interrupt-correct-repeat loop — feedback without a
  repeat request is advice, not coaching.
- CHRO and COO not coordinating at the weekly checkpoint — the people side and the
  process side can diverge; coordinate explicitly.
- Not ingesting the onboarding record to the KB — the onboarding history is the
  pattern library that makes the next hire cheaper and faster.
- Declaring autonomy before the 90%+ quality bar is met — autonomy without quality
  is abandonment.
