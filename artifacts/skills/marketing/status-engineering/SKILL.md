# Status Engineering

Flexible capability skill. Takes an existing offer, product or brand and re-engineers
its positioning so the target ICP sees the purchase as a move up in their status
game — not merely as a transaction.

Anchored on: Sabri Suby ("sell status, not stuff" — personas 775 lines, WizardingCode
vault); Alex Hormozi (identity-based positioning, premium pricing psychology — 169
sources); Russell Brunson (Attractive Character, tribe identity, belief systems — 799
lines); Neil Patel (premium brand signals, authority positioning). Complementary to:
`marketing:rebranding-execution` (the brand architecture decisions), `fin:pricing-
redesign` (the premium pricing implementation), `marketing:offer-architecture` (the
copy execution).

## Steps

### 1. KB-first

`kb_search` and `kb_ask` for:
- Company profile (`wzrdx company`) — current positioning, pricing tier, brand voice.
- Status Engineering framework from the KB (Suby persona — "sell status, not stuff").
- Rebranding-2026-06 vault — current positioning statement, brand architecture,
  verbal identity, and the 4 visual directions. All status decisions must be coherent
  with the locked-in brand direction.
- ICP persona data in the KB — if ICPs have been documented, read them first.
- Any prior positioning memos, brand audits, or pricing-redesign outputs.
- NUEPH framework — the "U" (Único) and "E" (Exclusivo) scores from any prior offer
  audit.

Surface what has been decided. Do not re-open closed brand architecture decisions
without a CEO balanced-decision gate.

### 2. Map the ICP's status game

Status is always relative to a reference group. Before repositioning anything,
identify the game the ICP is already playing:

**Step 1 — Identify the reference group:**
Who does the ICP compare themselves to? Their peers in the same vertical, their
competitors, their aspirational role models, or a specific tier they aspire to join?

**Step 2 — Identify the status markers they already value:**
What signals do they use to evaluate themselves and others in the reference group?
(E.g.: client roster, technology stack, team size, brand reputation, content reach,
certifications, event attendance, thought-leader associations.)

**Step 3 — Identify the status anxiety:**
What do they fear losing or failing to attain in their reference group? The status
anxiety is the emotional engine of the purchase — the offer must relieve it.

**Step 4 — Map the current product to the status game:**
Where does the current offer sit in the ICP's status map? Is it:
- **Below their radar** (not visible to the reference group — irrelevant to status)?
- **Neutral** (functional purchase — not a status signal either way)?
- **Status-positive** (buying it signals they are in the aspirational tier)?
- **Status-negative** (buying it would mark them as behind or low-status)?

Document the map explicitly before attempting repositioning.

### 3. Reposition as status artifact

A status artifact is a purchase that the ICP makes not just for its utility but for
what it signals to their reference group and to themselves.

Apply the three repositioning moves:

**Move 1 — Identity reframe:**
Shift the offer from "product/service that does X" to "what [aspirational tier of ICP]
use to achieve Y". The ICP buys an identity, not a solution.

Before: "AI workflow automation for SMEs."
After: "The operating system that AI-native founders in Portugal use to scale without
adding headcount."

Draft the identity reframe for the current offer. It must name the aspirational tier
and the outcome the ICP aspires to.

**Move 2 — Belonging signal:**
Make it clear that this offer is used by the people the ICP wants to be like. This
is the case study / social proof layer with a status lens:
- Whose name can be mentioned? (Client roster or persona associations — e.g. "used
  by the same methodology that [known persona] applies to their clients.")
- What exclusive group does the buyer join? (Cohort, community, early-access tier,
  advisory board — any structure that creates an in-group the ICP aspires to.)

**Move 3 — Exclusion signal:**
If anyone can buy it, it is not exclusive. Status requires that some people are out.
Explicit exclusion increases desire among the right buyers:
- "This is for founders running ≥ €50K/month."
- "We work with maximum 5 clients per quarter."
- "Application required."
- "By referral only."

Choose the exclusion mechanism that is truthful and that the company can operationalise.
Do not fake scarcity or exclusivity — it destroys trust when the ICP finds out.

Document all three moves as a **repositioning brief** (one page).

### 4. Apply Van Cleef-style premium signals

Premium is not only about price — it is about every sensory and cognitive signal the
brand sends. Van Cleef & Arpels charges 100× more than a functional jeweller not
because its products are 100× more useful but because every signal — from the box to
the receipt to the language of the sales associate — communicates rare craft and
exclusivity.

Audit and redesign the brand's signals across the ICP's journey:

| Touchpoint | Current signal | Premium signal | Priority |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|----------|
| Website first impression | ... | ... | H/M/L |
| Pricing presentation | ... | ... | ... |
| Proposal design | ... | ... | ... |
| Email language and cadence | ... | ... | ... |
| Onboarding experience | ... | ... | ... |
| Social media presence | ... | ... | ... |
| Case study framing | ... | ... | ... |
| Offboarding / completion | ... | ... | ... |

Premium signal principles:
- **Specificity over volume:** one specific, credible claim beats ten generic ones.
- **Confidence over hedging:** premium brands never apologise for their price or hedge
  their promises. "If you are the right client, this will deliver [result]."
- **Scarcity signals:** limited capacity, application process, waitlist, cohort-based
  intake — the difficulty of access signals value.
- **Language:** remove discount language, urgency faking, and mass-market phrasing.
  Premium language is calm, specific, and authoritative. It never begs.
- **Visual quality:** proposals, decks, and onboarding materials must match the
  positioning — a premium brand with a budget-looking proposal creates dissonance.

Coordinate visual signal improvements with the Rebranding-2026-06 visual direction
(CEO balanced-decision gate controls visual direction selection).

### 5. Align with brand voice and rebranding direction

Every status repositioning must be coherent with the current brand direction:

- Read the Rebranding-2026-06 verbal identity doc (from the KB or vault).
- Test the repositioning brief (§3) against the brand voice: does the language feel
  like the same brand? Does the identity reframe amplify the positioning v2 from the
  vault, or contradict it?
- If contradictions are found: flag them and route to the CMO + CEO for resolution.
  Do not reconcile a positioning contradiction unilaterally.
- If verbal identity is not yet locked (D5 pending): write in the interim voice —
  "clear, authoritative, no jargon, European Portuguese primary / English secondary"
  — and flag for verbal identity review once D5 is resolved.

### 6. Produce the status positioning memo

Consolidate the work into a one-page memo:

```
Status Positioning Memo — [Offer / Brand] — [YYYY-MM-DD]

ICP status game
Reference group: [who they compare to]
Status markers: [the signals they value]
Status anxiety: [what they fear losing]
Current product position: [below radar / neutral / status-positive / status-negative]

Repositioning brief
Identity reframe: [before → after]
Belonging signal: [the in-group and how to signal it]
Exclusion mechanism: [what makes it not-for-everyone]

Premium signal audit
[Top 3 touchpoints to upgrade — current signal → premium signal — owner — deadline]

Brand voice check
[Aligned / contradictions found. If contradictions: flag + route to CMO + CEO.]

Next action
Owner: [CMO / CEO]   Deadline: [YYYY-MM-DD]   Success criterion: [measurable]
```

Route the memo to the CMO for review. Flag any significant repositioning to the CEO
balanced-decision gate — a brand repositioning that changes the ICP target or the
price tier is a significant decision.

`kb_ingest` after every status engineering session:
- Title: `Status Positioning — [Offer/Brand] — [YYYY-MM-DD]`.
- Tags: `status-engineering`, `positioning`, `premium`, `brand`, `marketing`,

- Include: the ICP status map, the repositioning brief (three moves), the premium

## Output contract

Every status engineering session produces:
1. **ICP status map** — reference group, markers, anxiety, and current product
   position (§2).
2. **Repositioning brief** — identity reframe, belonging signal, exclusion mechanism
   (§3).
3. **Premium signal audit table** — top touchpoints with current → premium signal and
   priority (§4).
4. **Brand voice alignment check** — aligned or contradictions flagged (§5).
5. **Status positioning memo** — one-pager ready for CMO review (§6).
6. **Next action** — single highest-leverage status signal to upgrade this week.
   Owner, deadline, success criterion.

## Red flags

- Repositioning before reading the Rebranding-2026-06 vault — status engineering that
  contradicts the brand architecture decisions fragments the brand; always read first.
- Using exclusion mechanisms the company cannot operationalise — a "by referral only"
  claim that is not enforced destroys trust faster than no claim at all.
- Changing the ICP target without CEO deliberation — targeting a different status tier
  means a different market, different CAC, different content; that is a CEO gate decision.
- Applying premium signals without upgrading the proposal and onboarding experience —
  a premium brand that sends budget-looking proposals creates dissonance that kills the
  close.
- Letting price-based competition persist without addressing the status layer — every
  price objection is evidence that the status repositioning has not yet worked; use it
  as diagnostic data, not a reason to discount.
- Not ingesting the status memo — positioning decisions that are not documented revert;
  the memo is the anchor against regression.
