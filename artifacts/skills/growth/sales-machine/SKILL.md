# Sales Machine

Flexible capability skill. Takes a lead, an opportunity or a sales-process gap and
produces a structured, executable sales motion grounded in the Obsidian KB sales
frameworks. Adapts depth to context — a full-funnel build vs. a single deal
follow-up vs. an objection script.

Anchored on: Sabri Suby (Magic Lantern, application funnels, VSSL), Alex Hormozi
(Revenue Growth Equation, Either-Or Close, QATQ objection handling), Neil Patel
(speed-to-lead three-fix). All sourced from the WizardingCode vault (Topics — Sales
Frameworks 504 lines, Sales Funnel Architecture, Lead Generation & Conversion).

## Steps

### 1. KB-first

`kb_search` and `kb_ask` for:
- Company profile (`wzrdx company`) — offer, ICP, positioning, price point.
- Prior deal notes, win/loss analyses, or pipeline snapshots in the KB.
- Sales Frameworks topic (504 lines): Magic Lantern, Revenue Growth Equation, QATQ,
  Either-Or Close, speed-to-lead three-fix, 24-36 Month Buyer Maturation.
- Sales Funnel Architecture and Lead Generation & Conversion topics.
- Personas: Sabri Suby (775 lines), Alex Hormozi, Russell Brunson — pull the
  relevant framework for the current task.

Note what already exists (scripts, templates, prior decisions). Only author from
scratch for genuine gaps.

### 2. Magic Lantern trust funnel

The default sales architecture for warm or cold inbound leads. Apply this sequence:

**Step 1 — Godfather Offer (irresistible entry):**
- Craft an offer the prospect would feel foolish refusing: high perceived value,
  low risk, specific outcome, immediate usefulness.
- The offer must address the prospect's single biggest pain point in concrete terms
  (not features — outcomes: "you'll have X done by Y date").
- Include a risk reversal: money-back, free first session, pilot scope, or results
  guarantee ("if you don't get X, you pay nothing").
- Align the offer with CMO on messaging language; bring final pricing to CFO + CEO
  for gate approval before quoting.

**Step 2 — Value-first follow-up sequence:**
- Do not go silent after the initial contact. Run a value-first cadence:
  - Touch 1: deliver something genuinely useful (insight, mini-audit, relevant
    case study) — no ask.
  - Touch 2 (2d later): personalised follow-up referencing the prospect's specific
    situation — soft ask ("Would a 20-minute call make sense?").
  - Touch 3 (4d later): social proof + objection pre-emption — share a result
    relevant to their vertical.
  - Touch 4 (7d later): either-or close (see §5).
  - Repeat with decreasing cadence until verdict (yes / no / not now).
- Document the cadence as a POP and register it in the KB.

**Step 3 — Application step (instead of "book a call"):**
- Replace generic "book a call" CTAs with an application form or a 3-question
  qualifier: Who are you? What's your current situation? What outcome do you want?
- This pre-qualifies the prospect, increases commitment, and lets you prepare a
  targeted strategy session.
- Only invite high-fit applicants to the strategy session.

**Step 4 — Strategy session (the close meeting):**
- Frame as a diagnostic, not a sales call: "I'm going to show you exactly what we
  would do for your situation."
- Structure: discover pain (step §3) → diagnose root cause → prescribe solution →
  present the offer → handle objections → close or set follow-up.
- Time-box: 45–60 minutes. Do not let it drift.

### 3. Discovery and qualification

Run structured discovery before prescribing any solution. Questions to answer:

| Dimension | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| Pain | What is the single most urgent problem you're trying to solve? |
| Impact | What does it cost you (€ or time) to leave it unsolved? |
| Timeline | When do you need this solved? What happens if it slips? |
| Authority | Who else needs to be involved in this decision? |
| Budget | Do you have a budget allocated? What range? |
| Fit | Have you tried other solutions? What happened? |

**Qualification threshold:** advance only prospects who have a clear pain, a real
timeline, decision authority, and budget signal. Log the rest as "nurture" in the
CRM — they belong to the 24-36 Month Buyer Maturation cadence.

### 4. Objection handling (QATQ and Either-Or)

**QATQ — Question About The Question (Hormozi):**
When an objection surfaces, do not answer it directly. Instead:
1. Acknowledge: "I hear you."
2. Ask a clarifying question that reveals the real concern: "Help me understand —
   is that a concern about [X] or about [Y]?"
3. Answer the revealed concern with evidence (case study, guarantee, specific data).
4. Confirm resolution: "Does that address it, or is there still something holding
   you back?"

Common objections and QATQ responses:
- "It's too expensive" → "Is it a budget constraint, or are you unsure about the
  ROI?" → present payback timeline or risk reversal.
- "I need to think about it" → "Of course — what specifically would you need to see
  to feel confident moving forward?" → surface the real objection.
- "I need to check with [person]" → "Absolutely — when can I join that conversation
  to answer their questions directly?"

**Either-Or Close (Hormozi):**
When the prospect is stalling, present two options only — both of which move them
forward:
- "Would you prefer to start with the full programme, or would a pilot scope first
  give you more confidence?"
- "Do you want to kick off this month while we have the slot, or should I hold it
  for next month and follow up then?"
Never offer open-ended options. Either-Or forces a decision without pressure.

### 5. Proposal and risk reversal guarantee

Structure every written proposal as follows:

1. **Current situation** — mirror back the prospect's pain in their words.
2. **Root cause** — your diagnosis of why the pain exists.
3. **Proposed solution** — specific deliverables, timeline, team, process.
4. **Expected outcomes** — concrete results (not activity), with a timeline.
5. **Investment** — clear pricing, payment terms, what is and is not included.
6. **Risk reversal** — guarantee or pilot structure that removes the prospect's
   downside: "If you don't achieve [X] within [Y] weeks, we [refund / extend /
   redo at no charge]."
7. **Next step** — single, specific action: "Sign the proposal by [date] and we
   start [date]."

Bring the proposal draft to CFO + CEO for pricing review before sending if the
deal size is above the pre-agreed threshold.

### 6. Follow-up cadence until verdict

A deal without a follow-up cadence is a deal left on the table. Rules:

- Every interaction ends with a defined next step and a next-contact date logged in
  the CRM.
- Default cadence post-proposal: Day 1 (check receipt), Day 3 (any questions?),
  Day 7 (value touch — relevant insight), Day 14 (either-or close).
- If the prospect goes silent: three more touches at 7-day intervals, then move to
  the 24-36 Month Buyer Maturation nurture sequence (do not delete — they buy
  eventually or refer someone who does).
- Speed-to-lead: inbound leads are contacted within 5 minutes (Neil Patel
  three-fix). This single habit has a disproportionate impact on conversão.

### 7. Ingest deal learnings to KB

After each deal closes (won or lost), ingest a deal debrief via `kb_ingest`:
- Title: `Deal Debrief — [Company] — [Won/Lost] — [YYYY-MM-DD]`.
- Tags: `deal`, `sales`, `won` or `lost`, `<vertical>`, `<company-slug>`.
- Include: prospect profile, pain, objections raised, how they were handled,
  close/loss reason, what to do differently.

Win/loss debriefs accumulate into the case study library. After 3+ wins in the
same vertical, draft a formal case study (CRO + CMO collaboration).

## Output contract

The deliverable always contains:
1. **Funnel architecture** — which Magic Lantern steps apply to this context and why.
2. **Scripts / templates** — ready-to-use copy for each active funnel step (Godfather
   Offer, follow-up touches, application questions, strategy-session agenda, proposal
   structure).
3. **Objection map** — the 3-5 most likely objections with QATQ responses and
   Either-Or closes.
4. **CRM actions** — what to log, which stage to move to, what the next-contact
   trigger is.
5. **Next action** — single most important commercial action right now, owner, deadline.

## Red flags

- Quoting a price before completing discovery — never price before diagnosing.
- Skipping the KB-first step — prior deal notes and frameworks already exist; use them.
- Following up without a defined next step — "I'll be in touch" is not a next step.
- Missing the risk reversal — without a guarantee, the prospect absorbs all the
  downside; conversão will stay below 15%.
- Not ingesting deal debriefs — sales intelligence compounds only if it is documented.
- Setting pricing without CFO + CEO gate — pricing policy is a joint decision.
- Speed-to-lead > 5 minutes for inbound — the single easiest conversão lever;
  never leave it unattended.
